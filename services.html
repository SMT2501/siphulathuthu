OT Cybersecurity & IT/OT Convergence

The integration of information technology (IT) systems with operational technology (OT) systems is becoming increasingly vital due to diverse business requirements and functionalities. In utility sectors, IT systems serve a variety of purposes, such as providing internet access, managing workforce scheduling, and generating reports. Conversely, OT systems collect and monitor operational data at the substation and control center levels, thereby ensuring the efficient functioning of the electricity grid. To protect the critical elements of the OT network, secure integration of IT and OT systems is essential.

Insights from Grid-India highlight that effective IT-OT integration starts with clearly defining expected outcomes, identifying challenges, evaluating implementation feasibility, enabling necessary restricted processes, creating standard operating procedures, conducting regular cyber audits, and addressing vulnerabilities. Numerous guidelines and regulations govern power utilities and delineate essential cybersecurity measures, including the Guidelines on Cyber Security (NIST Cybersecurity Framework, NISTIR 7626 and 7628), Technical Security Controls and Techniques (IEC 62443-4-2, IEC 62351 series), IEC 62351-12, Internet Standards (IPSec RFC 1827, TLS RFC 5246), as well as National Electricity Grid Codes and National Electricity Plans.

Beyond these guidelines and regulations, adopting relevant ISO certifications, such as ISO 27001, is crucial to ensure that all IT-OT integrations adhere to established standards. ISO 27001 is widely implemented in IT contexts, while the NIST Framework and Internet Standards (IPSec and TLS) are relevant in both IT and OT environments. Additionally, IEC standards are increasingly being embraced within OT settings.

Digitization & Sustainability Training

This program serves as an introductory module for Information Systems, Telecommunications, and Cybersecurity, specifically targeting TVET participants with an NQF4 qualification. The modules are crucial for those looking to grasp ICT-related concepts, with a particular emphasis on Telecommunications, Networking, and Cybersecurity. Practical components are integrated into the course outline to enhance the learning experience.

The course objectives are designed to equip participants with the following knowledge and skills:

  • An understanding of basic cybersecurity concepts, focusing on the human element and risks associated with internet usage, including training on phishing, vishing, and more.
  • An introduction to Networking and fundamental Information Technology concepts.
  • An overview of Telecommunications and basic Operational Technology.
  • Insights into Digitization and the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR).
  • Awareness of global ICT trends and Africa's position in the international landscape.
  • Fundamental skills in configuring networking and security devices, such as switches, routers, servers, and firewalls.
  • Knowledge to assemble Telecommunication devices and a thorough understanding of the function of each piece of equipment.
  • Practical cybersecurity training using videos and other resources to help participants recognize and understand cybersecurity risks.
